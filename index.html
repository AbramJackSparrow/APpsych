AP Psychology Brain Map

Outer Brain (Cerebral Lobes & Cortex)

Frontal Lobes: Planning, problem solving, movement, and personality.
Parietal Lobes: Process sensory input for touch and body position.
Occipital Lobes: Responsible for visual processing.
Temporal Lobes: Process hearing and memory information.
Somatosensory Cortex: Receives and interprets sensory signals from the body.
Motor Cortex: Controls voluntary muscle movements.
Broca’s Area: Controls speech production and language expression.
Wernicke’s Area: Involved in understanding spoken language.
Cerebellum: Coordinates movement, posture, and balance.

AP Psychology Interactive Brain Map

Outer Brain (Lobes & Cortexes)

Cerebrum
Responsible for cognitive processing, motor control, and emotional regulation. Includes the major lobes.
Frontal Lobes
Decision-making, reasoning, and voluntary movement.
Parietal Lobes
Process touch, temperature, and spatial information.
Temporal Lobes
Hearing, language comprehension, and memory.
Occipital Lobes
Vision, color, and depth perception.
Somatosensory Cortex
Processes sensory information like touch and temperature.
Motor Cortex
Controls voluntary movements.
Broca’s Area
Speech production and language processing.
Wernicke’s Area
Understanding written and spoken language.
Olfactory Bulb
Processes the sense of smell.
Cerebellum
Balance, coordination, and fine motor control.

Inner Brain (Internal Structures)

Amygdala
Processes emotions like fear and pleasure.
Thalamus
Relays sensory and motor signals to the cortex.
Hypothalamus
Controls hunger, thirst, and body temperature.
Hippocampus
Forms and stores long-term memories.
Pituitary Gland
Regulates hormones and other endocrine glands.
Medulla (Oblongata)
Controls heartbeat, breathing, and reflexes.
Corpus Callosum
Connects the brain’s hemispheres.
Pons
Relays signals between the cerebrum and cerebellum.
Basal Ganglia
Coordinates movement and habit formation.
